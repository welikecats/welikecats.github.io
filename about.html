History of Trampolining

The Trampoline was invented by George Nissen is 1936, it was originally used mostly to train airline pilots and navigators to give them pratice in getting used to having their bodies in various positions in space.

Trampolining first became on Olympic Sport at the Sydney Olympics in the year 2000. In the modern day a competition involves each competitior completing two routines of 10 moves, which must be completed consecutively without any bounces in between. Competitiors get points based on both the execution of the moves and also the difficult similarly to gymnastics.
